reset() vs restart() — what each touches, per track

Both put the playhead back to the start. The difference is everything else. Green = restart keeps it (continuity). Red = only reset() also clears it (the cold extra).

What this means for "warm the Reset target." It lands clean on PhaseFlux (built for it), Note, Curve, DiscreteMap. It's questionable on Stochastic (every sync re-seeds the RNG → you replay the same stochastic phrase) and Indexed (restart still dumps CV and re-rolls the advance — not gentle). It does nothing different on Tuesday (restart == reset, still cold) and MidiCv (nothing to anchor). So a real "warm re-anchor everywhere" needs new restart() work on Tuesday + Indexed (and a decision on Stochastic's RNG), not just a one-line routing swap.

The common axis

Across tracks, the cold-only extras cluster into four things restart() tends to preserve and reset() always wipes: (1) live CV/gate output, (2) pending queues, (3) accumulators / evolving counters, (4) changePattern() / re-latch the pattern. "Warm" = keep 1–3, skip 4. The tracks differ only in how completely their restart() honors that.
