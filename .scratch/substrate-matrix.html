XFORMER vs Sequoa: Shared Sequencer Substrate Research

Executive Verdict

A Sequoa-style StageCrosser (unified fraction/weight time core) is fundamentally incompatible with XFORMER's current architecture without severe RAM bloat and semantic breakage. XFORMER utilizes four distinct time-progression primitives: Grid/Tick (Note/Curve), Duration-Sum (Indexed/Stochastic), Voltage-Threshold (DiscreteMap), and Cumulative-Fraction (PhaseFlux). Attempting to unify these into one time engine would force polymorphism or massive union states in the 1kHz loop.

However, the Value/Scale Projection math is highly duplicated and represents the safest, most immediate extraction candidate. The identical scale projection math is copy-pasted across 5 engine files. Gate Event Queuing is a shared structural pattern, but since payload semantics differ per track, it remains a deferred extraction.

1. Proven Common Primitives

  • Degree-to-Voltage Readout: The block scale.noteToVolts(note + shift) + (scale.isChromatic() ? rootNote / 12.f : 0.f) is strictly duplicated in Note, Indexed, PhaseFlux, Stochastic, and Tuesday.
  • Slew Application: Slide::applySlide() is universally used for time-based exponential smoothing across Note, Curve, Indexed, and Teletype.

2. False Commonalities

  • "Stage" and "Tick Position": A stage in NoteTrack is a linear grid cell. A stage in DiscreteMap is a voltage window. A stage in Indexed is a duration sum. A stage in PhaseFlux is a fractional slice of a cumulative measure. They share the word "stage" but share zero mathematical placement logic.
  • Accumulators: NoteTrack's Accumulator object (global/local UI) is entirely different from PhaseFlux's array of AccumulatorOps (Wrap/Pendulum bounds logic) and Stochastic's simple cycle-end _jumpRegister.

3. Missing Substrate Concepts (Sequoa vs XFORMER)

  • Generalized Crosser: Sequoa abstracts all time into a unified fraction 0.0-1.0 over weights. XFORMER has no central time abstraction; each engine builds its own tick/phase loops.
  • Unified Readout Pipeline: Sequoa pushes everything through one projection layer. XFORMER engines do their own pitch math.

4. XFORMER Features Sequoa Cannot Express

  • Overlapping Absolute Child Events: Event queues outlive their parent steps. Sequoa's advanceAt relies on stage dwell, breaking if ratchets span stage boundaries.
  • Voltage-Domain Triggers: DiscreteMap's rising/falling CV threshold partitioning.
  • Generative VMs: Teletype script environments and Tuesday's C-struct gesture machines.

5. Recommended First Extraction

PitchReadout (Scale/Degree Voltage Helper): Extract strictly the degree + octave/transpose -> scale.noteToVolts(...) + chromatic root offset math into a stateless helper namespace. This prevents accumulator/slew/order creep, affects 5 files, and poses zero risk of breaking 1kHz timing or RAM limits.

6. Rejected / Deferred Extractions

  • StageCrosser / Time Substrate: Rejected. Attempting to merge Indexed's while-loop, Note's tick-modulo, and PhaseFlux's linear cumulative scan would require a massive TrackEngine union size increase (violating RAM constraints) and would obliterate clean, localized logic.
  • Gate Queues (SortedQueue): Deferred. While Note, Stochastic, Tuesday, and Curve share the SortedQueue shape, their payloads and semantics differ (e.g., Note carries accumulator/CV metadata, Tuesday carries CV per micro-gate). This is a shared structural pattern, not a universal shared primitive yet.

7. Verification Strategy

  • Focused Parity Tests: For the first tiny extraction (PitchReadout), skip the full golden-output trace harness. Instead, add focused parity unit tests around each caller to prove exact math equivalence.
  • Deferred Golden Traces: Reserve the heavy "golden output trace" harness for future extractions that touch timing, SortedQueue operations, accumulators, or scheduler order.
