Concept 2: Tidal Bloom

Inspiration: spirals.lua (organic outward growth) + flora.lua (L-system branching) + dunes.lua (function topography).
Poetic framing "Each note is a droplet hitting water. The surface remembers every impact as a ring that drifts leftward and fades. Pitch is the splash height. Rests are still water."

How it works

  • Water line: a faint baseline runs through the viewport center (the "surface").
  • Ripples: each non-rest event creates a concentric ring that propagates leftward over time, fading as it travels. The ring amplitude = pitch height (CV).
  • Newest event: at the right edge, the ring is still expanding — a bright arc that hasn't fully opened.
  • Older events: their rings have drifted left and dimmed to faint ellipses.
  • Rests: no ring is cast. The surface remains flat and undisturbed at that time position.
  • Burst children: harmonic ripples — smaller, faster-decay rings stacked inside the parent ring.
  • Slide: connects the peaks of overlapping rings with a smooth curve (a "wavefront").
  • Legato: sustains the water line between two peaks, making the surface feel continuously excited.
  • Contour: tilts the entire water plane, so rings appear to flow uphill or downhill.

Why this works

  • Time is encoded as horizontal drift — intuitive and organic.
  • Pitch becomes splash height — the eye reads amplitude instantly.
  • Rests create flat stillness, making silence visible as negative space.
  • Burst children read as harmonics/overtones, not technical scheduling.
  • The wavefront line gives melodic contour without needing dots.
