Concept 1: Constellation Draft

Inspiration: constellations.lua (star field + glow + connection lines) + luck.lua (node topology).
Poetic framing "The sequencer is a telescope. Each played note is a star. Consecutive phrases draw constellations across the dark."

How it works

  • Background: faint cosmic dust — scattered single pixels, density modulated by spread.
  • Stars: each engine event becomes a star. Vertical position = pitch (CV). Horizontal = time, newest at right edge.
  • Brightness: newest star burns at full white with a glow halo; older stars fade through gray tiers.
  • Constellation lines: consecutive non-rest events are connected by faint threads. slide thickens the thread into a luminous ribbon.
  • Rests: drawn as dim hollow circles (ghost stars) or simply omitted, leaving gaps in the constellation.
  • Burst children: small companion stars clustered near the parent, offset by burstRate spacing.
  • Targeting reticle: the newest event has a crosshair + pulse animation (like the constellations targeting computer).
  • Pitch rails: remain as faint horizontal guides, but drawn as dashed rather than solid.

Why this works

  • Every event has a natural place: pitch = altitude, time = longitude.
  • Rests create negative space (gaps in constellations) which is visually legible.
  • Burst reads as natural star clusters, not technical child-events.
  • The targeting reticle gives the newest event a "hero" status without numeric readouts.
